# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Keshav Memorial Institute of Technology (KMIT) is committed to providing a respectful, inclusive, and professional environment for all members of our academic community. This Code of Conduct applies to all participants in the KMIT Projects repository and related spaces, including but not limited to:

- Pull requests and code reviews
- Issue discussions
- Comments and feedback
- All interactions within GitHub repositories
- Related communication channels

We are dedicated to fostering a community where every student, faculty member, and contributor feels valued, respected, and empowered to contribute their best work.

---

## Our Values

### 🤝 Respect

We treat all members of our community with dignity and respect, regardless of their background, experience level, or perspective.

- Listen actively to others' viewpoints
- Acknowledge different ideas and approaches
- Value diverse perspectives and experiences
- Respond constructively to feedback

### 🌈 Inclusiveness

We welcome contributors from all backgrounds and strive to create an inclusive environment.

- Respect differences in gender, race, ethnicity, age, religion, nationality, sexual orientation, and disability
- Actively encourage participation from underrepresented groups
- Ensure our language and interactions are welcoming to all
- Adapt our communication styles for accessibility

### 💼 Professional Behavior

We maintain high standards of professionalism in all interactions.

- Communicate clearly and courteously
- Focus on ideas and work, not personal attacks
- Maintain professional language in all discussions
- Acknowledge others' contributions appropriately
- Take disagreements constructively

### 🎓 Academic Integrity

Academic honesty and integrity are fundamental to our institution and this repository.

- Submit only your own original work
- Do not plagiarize code, documentation, or ideas from others
- Properly attribute external resources, libraries, and inspirations
- Do not submit work completed by others as your own
- Understand that violations may result in academic consequences

**What constitutes plagiarism:**
- Copying code from others without attribution or modification
- Submitting projects not written by you
- Using online solutions without learning and implementing yourself
- Failing to cite sources and inspirations
- Paraphrasing others' work without attribution

**Proper attribution:**
```markdown
# Inspired by [Project Name] by [Author Name]
# Reference: [Link to original work]

# Special thanks to [Person/Resource Name] for guidance
```

### 🤐 Responsible Collaboration

We work together as a community with shared goals of learning and growth.

- Share knowledge and help teammates succeed
- Mentor and support less experienced contributors
- Collaborate openly and transparently
- Acknowledge when you don't know something
- Ask for help when needed
- Respect others' time and boundaries

## Expected Behavior

### In Code and Documentation

✅ **Do:**
- Write clear, well-documented code
- Use meaningful variable and function names
- Include helpful comments explaining complex logic
- Write descriptive commit messages
- Provide comprehensive README files
- Give credit to contributors and sources

✅ **Be:**
- Constructive in code reviews
- Patient with learning developers
- Respectful of different coding styles
- Open to feedback and suggestions
- Willing to help others understand your code

### In Pull Requests and Issues

✅ **Do:**
- Follow the provided templates
- Provide clear descriptions of your work
- Ask clarifying questions respectfully
- Respond promptly to feedback
- Acknowledge and thank reviewers
- Help review others' work

✅ **Be:**
- Detailed and specific in your descriptions
- Patient during the review process
- Grateful for constructive criticism
- Professional in your language
- Responsive to maintainers

### In Discussions and Comments

✅ **Do:**
- Stay on topic and relevant to the discussion
- Provide evidence or reasoning for your statements
- Ask for clarification if you don't understand something
- Acknowledge when others make good points
- Share knowledge and experiences
- Support and encourage your peers

✅ **Be:**
- Respectful and courteous
- Constructive and helpful
- Open-minded to different perspectives
- Humble and willing to learn
- Positive and encouraging

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are strictly prohibited and will not be tolerated:

### ❌ Harassment and Intimidation
- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments about any person
- Intimidation, bullying, or threats
- Sustained disruption of discussions or work
- Unwelcome comments about someone's identity, appearance, or background
- Repeated unwanted contact or aggressive communication

### ❌ Discriminatory Conduct
- Discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability, age, or nationality
- Sexist, racist, or other offensive language or imagery
- Excluding people based on protected characteristics
- Stereotyping or making assumptions about individuals

### ❌ Offensive Content
- Hate speech or slurs
- Sexually inappropriate comments or advances
- Graphic or violent content
- Obscene or abusive language
- Content that violates others' dignity or creates hostile environment

### ❌ Academic Dishonesty
- Submitting plagiarized code or documentation
- Claiming credit for others' work
- Cheating or attempting to circumvent academic policies
- Helping others commit plagiarism or fraud
- Misrepresenting authorship or contributions

### ❌ Inappropriate Use of Repository
- Uploading sensitive information (passwords, API keys, personal data)
- Malicious code or intentional security vulnerabilities
- Spam or unsolicited advertisements
- Intentional disruption of repository functionality
- Unauthorized access to others' work or information

### ❌ Privacy Violations
- Sharing others' personal information without consent
- Publishing private communications without permission
- Doxing or revealing someone's personal details
- Violating others' privacy or creating safety concerns

### ❌ Other Misconduct
- Deliberate obstruction or sabotage of others' work
- Retaliation against those who report violations
- Attempted circumvention of these policies
- Conduct that otherwise violates institutional policies

## Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, we encourage you to report it. Your safety and the community's wellbeing are our priorities.

### How to Report

**Option 1: Contact Your Course Instructor**
- Speak with your course faculty member
- They can facilitate resolution and support
- Appropriate for academic integrity concerns

**Option 2: Contact Repository Maintainers**
- Email: contact@kmit.in
- Subject: "[URGENT] Code of Conduct Violation Report"
- Provide details of the incident

**Option 3: Contact KMIT Administration**
- Visit KMIT official website: https://kmit.in/
- Contact your department's student affairs office
- Appropriate for serious violations

### What to Include in Your Report

Please provide the following information:

1. **Your Information** (can be confidential)
   - Your name and contact information
   - Your relationship to the repository

2. **Incident Details**
   - Description of what happened
   - Date and time of the incident
   - Location (which PR, issue, comment, etc.)

3. **Involved Parties**
   - Names or usernames of involved individuals
   - Any relevant GitHub links

4. **Supporting Information**
   - Screenshots or direct links to evidence
   - Witness information (if applicable)
   - Any previous reports of similar behavior

### Confidentiality

- Your report will be handled confidentially
- We will not disclose reporter identity without consent
- Exceptions: when necessary for safety or legal compliance
- You are protected from retaliation for good-faith reports

### Investigation Process

1. We will acknowledge receipt of your report
2. A review will be conducted to gather facts
3. All parties involved will be contacted
4. We will determine appropriate action
5. You will be informed of the outcome (as appropriate)
6. Follow-up support will be offered

## Enforcement

The KMIT administration and repository maintainers are responsible for enforcing this Code of Conduct. Violations will be addressed fairly and consistently.

### Possible Consequences

Depending on severity and context, violations may result in:

1. **Verbal Warning**
   - First-time minor violations
   - Discussion and education about expectations

2. **Written Warning**
   - Repeated violations or concerning behavior
   - Documented in student file if applicable

3. **Temporary Suspension**
   - Serious violations or pattern of misconduct
   - Removal from repository access for specified period

4. **Permanent Ban**
   - Severe violations or repeated serious offenses
   - Permanent removal from repository and platform
   - Referral to institutional disciplinary process

5. **Institutional Action**
   - Violations involving plagiarism or academic dishonesty
   - Academic penalties as per KMIT policies
   - Referral to disciplinary committee

### Appeal Process

If you believe a consequence was unjust, you may appeal:

1. Submit appeal to KMIT administration within 10 days
2. Provide clear explanation of why consequence should be reconsidered
3. An independent review will be conducted
4. Decision will be communicated to you

## Guidance for Reviewers

As a code reviewer or faculty member, you play a crucial role in maintaining our community standards.

### In Your Reviews

- Provide constructive feedback focused on improvement
- Acknowledge and praise good work
- Ask questions to help others learn
- Model professional and respectful behavior
- Address violations promptly and fairly
- Support contributors through the review process

### Difficult Conversations

When addressing concerns:

1. **Be specific** — Reference exact code, messages, or behavior
2. **Be private** — Discuss sensitive issues in private channels when appropriate
3. **Be respectful** — Focus on the behavior, not the person
4. **Be fair** — Consider context and intent
5. **Be supportive** — Offer guidance on improvement
6. **Be consistent** — Apply standards uniformly

### Recognizing Good Contributions

- Thank contributors for their work
- Recognize excellent code or documentation
- Highlight positive community contributions
- Share appreciation publicly when appropriate
- Mentor and encourage growth
